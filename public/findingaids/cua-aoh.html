Acquisition Information

This collection was donated in several stages over the course of 23 years, with the first microfilm deposit from James O'Connor taking place in 1994. After a gap of ten years, the materials that came to comprise series 2 of this collection were donated by the Ladies Auxiliary of Warren County, New Jersey. Smaller subsequent deposits from John Meehan, President, took place in 2007 and 2008, and came to form series 3. Additional miscellaneous material received in 2015 and 2016 were absorbed into series 1 and 3 as well as formed series 4. One small deposit was made by Mike McCormack, National Historian, in 2017 and was added to series 4.

Historical Note

The Ancient Order of Hibernians (AOH) was founded in 1836 in order to provide assistance of various forms to Irish immigrants in the United States. Originally a secret society, the first American chapters of the Order sprung up in New York City and in the coal-mining area of Pottsville, Pennsylvania, where Irish workers faced significant discrimination. At that time, the order was largely preoccupied with the protection of church property and the provision of social welfare benefits to its members.

At its inception, the AOH in the United States had been closely tied with its sister organization in Ireland. This changed as the American wing grew dissatisfied with satellite governance from the Irish order; the majority of US chapters formed what came to be officially known as the Ancient Order of Hibernians in America in 1884. Although some chapters were initially opposed to the split, they eventually unified under the US AOH banner in 1898.

In 1894, the Daughters of Erin were founded as a means for allowing female participation in the activities of the AOH. The AOH connection was made explicit in 1906, with the renaming of the organization to "Ladies Auxiliary to the Ancient Order of Hibernians in America."

Although no longer a secret society, the AOH exists today as a fraternal organization for Catholics of Irish birth or descent, although its current membership base is comprised mostly of those with Irish ancestry. Its activities during the 20th and 21st centuries have included: lobbying on behalf of the Irish nationalist movement and helping form the Irish national caucus in 1974; publication of the AOH newspaper, The National Hibernian Digest; and the continuing promotion of Irish culture through educational programs.

Scope and Contents

This collection consists of five series: The Ancient Order of Hibernians Records; The Ladies' Auxiliary to the Ancient Order of Hibernians Records of Warren County, Phillipsburg, New Jersey; Digital Ephemera; Newspapers; and Oversize Items.

Series 1: The Ancient Order of Hibernians Records contains convention proceedings from the 1960s, membership flyers and event programs spanning the early 1970s to the early 1980s, publications from the 2000s, as well as photographs spanning from the 2008 to 2015. Note that these materials are not comprehensive, and several years are missing due to the nature of deposit.

Series 2: The Ladies' Auxiliary to the Ancient Order of Hibernians Records of Warren County, Phillipsburg, New Jersey contains completed membership applications from 1943 to 1960, a treasurer's account ledger with notes from 1974 to 1996, meeting minutes from 1992 to 1996, and a written history of the LAAOH of Phillipsburg, New Jersey.

Series 3: Digital Ephemera contains directories of AOH officers from 1884 and 1902 through 1904, national convention press clippings and photographs, constitutional revisions, miscellaneous images, a collection of images of Hibernian stained glass windows from around the United States, National History Day presentations on DVD, and other Irish history resources on DVD. Please note that all file names are original and have not been altered.

Series 4: Newspapers contains several Ancient Order of Hibernian related publication from 1878 through 2015 in several formats, including compact disc, microfilm, and print. The earliest publication is the Ancient Order of Hibernians Journal - Richmond, VA., 1878, available on compact disc. The National Hibernian Digest is available on microfilm for the years 1905 through 1914 and 1932 through 1993, in print for the years 1966 through 2015, and on compact disc for the years 2002 to 2007 and 2014 to 2016. The Ladies Hibernian News is available on compact disc for the years 2002 through 2003. The Irish People is available in print for the years 1972 through 1987. There are also a few print copies of and clippings from The Irish Echo, 2008 through 2014.

Series 5: Oversize Items contains a framed charter of the Ladies' Auxiliary to the Ancient Order of Hibernians of Phillipsburg, New Jersey, an Irish Catholic Fraternity easel for display of flyers, and an Ancient Order of Hibernians in America, District of Columbia banner.
