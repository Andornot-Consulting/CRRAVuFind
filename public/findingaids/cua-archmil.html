Historical Note

The Archdiocese for the Military Services, USA, is a relatively new institution, but one with a longer history. The current structure was formed by Saint Pope John Paul II in 1985, but its origins go back to 1917, when a Military Vicariate was established within the United States, with then-auxiliary Archbishop of New York Patrick J. Hayes serving as vicar, a position he kept even after becoming Archbishop of New York in 1919.

The current Archbishop, Timothy P. Broglio, J. C. D., assisted by four auxiliary bishops, oversees chaplains serving Catholic stationed at more than 220 military installations in 29 countries, as well as tending to the needs of patients in 153 V. A. Medical Centers and government employees serving abroad in 134 countries, as well as their families. Overall, the Archdioceses tends to the spiritual needs of 1.8 million people, both adults and children.

The Military Archdioceses for Military Services, USA is not a government organization, nor is it funded in any part by the United States government. The Archdiocese is, however, the sole endorser of Roman Catholic chaplains for either the military service or V.A. hospitals. A Catholic priest must receive the endorsement of the Archdiocese in order to serve within the United States Military. The chaplains are not incardinated into the AMS, but rather remain within their home diocese or religious order, and are considered "on loan" to the Archdiocese. The AMS also does not have parishes; all chapels are the property of the US government. Rather than keeping parish registries, then, the Archdiocese keeps a centralized Office of Sacramental Records to record baptisms, first communions, confirmations, and marriages celebrated on military installations. These records currently number more than 2.5 million

This collection is comprised almost entirely in records created for public consumption by the Archdioceses for the Military Services, USA. The AMS produces a number of newsletters and magazines, three of which (AMS News, Chaplain's Newsletter, Salute Magazine) are significantly represented in this collection. Also included are assorted memos, prayer and liturgy aids, public notices, and video records of public addresses and masses.

Scope and Contents

The first series is Administrative Records, 1997-2014, with assorte official policies, announcements, and worship aids produces by the Archdiocese for the Military Services USA.

The second series is Publications, 1945(1983-2015)2015, which is further divided into two subseries. The first subseries is Publications of the Archdiocese, containing the Archdiocese's News Service, Chaplain's Newsletter, and Magazine, as well as assorted calendars, prayer aids, pamphlets, and more. The second subseries is Miscellaneous Publications, comprised of materials related to military chaplaincy and the religious needs of military service members not produced by the Archdioceses.

The third series is Audio/Visual Records, 2003-2004, and undated, comprising boxes 4-5. It is an assortment of lectures and masses performed under the AMS, as well as a few incidental items produced by the AMS.
