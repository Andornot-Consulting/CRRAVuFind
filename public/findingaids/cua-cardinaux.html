Descriptive Summary

Title: Autographes des cardinaux des XVII, XVIII, et XIX siecles
Dates: 1653-1879
Abstract: The Autographes de cardinaux des XVII, XVIII, et XIX siècle is primarily a compilation of the correspondence of Cardinals in the Catholic Church originating between 1653-1879 and bound in a leather tome. Also contained in the collection are a small number of prints of portraits of the cardinals, newspaper clippings, and certificates. All of the materials in the collection are written in Italian, French, or Latin. The majority of the correspondence is written in Italian.
Extent: 2.5 inches; 1 box
Language: Italian, French, and Latin
Identification: ACUA 306

Administrative Information

Acquisition Information

Collected and donated by Right Reverend Arthur T. Connolly, a Boston priest (1853-1933) who served as a trustee of the Boston Public Library. An avid collector of rare pieces of art and texts, he gave over 100 items to Catholic University, which included 92 pieces of ivory, 11 other artistic pieces, 20 rare books, and 10 manuscripts.

Historical Note

The contents of the collection, including correspondence and other documents, are somewhat eclectic. Chronologically, the correspondence contains a wide range - the first correspondence dating from 1653 and the last from 1879. Perhaps the most obvious theme of the collection is the concentration on Catholic cardinals. Although no other basis for the collection is easily discernable, it is worthy to note that 79 of the 137 cardinals (or roughly fifty-eight percent) lived through or were involved in the Napoleonic era of European politics. Many of the cardinals were exiled from France or Italy during the first decade of the nineteenth century. Cardinals Fesch, Micara, Opizzoni, Vidoni, Zauli, and Zondadari were among many from France and Italy directly affected by Napoleon's reign.

Scope and Content

The Autographes de cardinaux des XVII, XVIII, et XIX siècle consist of 134 personal correspondence and certificates of Catholic Cardinals dating from 1653 to 1879. There are two prints of portraits for the cardinals included with their correspondence and a newspaper clipping, which correlates the author of one of the correspondence. All of these documents are bound by a glue substance in a large book containing an index of the Cardinals' letters and biographical notes, either written ont the page the document is glued to or on a loose, index card sized piece of paper, on most of the Cardinals with varying degrees of detail and in some instances mistaken identities of authors of the correspondence. Geographically, the letters' origins range between various cities in Italy, France, and Germany with the majority written in Rome. The letters are arranged alphabetically (not chronologically) by the Cardinals' names although arbitrarily based on surname, given name, or place name with some out of order. Some of the letters are damaged, typically with the name of the receiver excised from the page; however, the authors' signatures remain intact. Some of the letters and certificates retain original wax or dry seals. The entirety of the collection is written in Italian, French, or Latin. Because the volume was edited, including the biographical notes and index, by an unknown compiler in French, the French versions of the names have been retained. The majority of the correspondence was written in Italian with some in French and Latin, and all official documents, such as certificates, were written in Latin.

The recto and verso of the first two pages include the index information with the second page marked as page one. Dates in Parentheses refer to dates of birth and death. Locations refer to the locations of authors presented in correspondence. All documents are correspondence unless labeled otherwise.
