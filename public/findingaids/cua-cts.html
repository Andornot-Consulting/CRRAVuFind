Descriptive Summary

Creator: College Theology Society, 1953-2015
Title: The College Theology Society (CTS) Records
Dates: 1953-2015
Abstract: Founded in 1954 as a Roman Catholic organization and professional association of college and university professors. Membership is open to those who teach and hold degrees in theology and religious studies with persons from the United States, Canada, and Europe. Members include William Cenker, Gary Macy, Miriam Ward, Francis Buckley, and Dennis Doyle. Records contain the constitutions and by-laws, meetings minutes, financial records, reports, awards, general correspondence, and audio-visual material, as well as material from the Annual Conventions, committees, elections, various geographical regions of CTS, and other theological societies. There are also a number of records on publications, such as bulletins, newsletters, books, and Annual Volumes.
Extent: 29 linear feet; 23 boxes
Language: English, Hebrew

Historical Note

Theology had a marginal status as an academic discipline for undergraduates until the mid-twentieth century. Most colleges and universities offered undergraduate courses that taught religion rather than theology, which incited clerics and members of religious orders to create the national organization, the Society of Catholic College Teachers of Sacred Doctrine in 1954. Most of the proceedings from the first to fifth annual meetings are recorded in published volumes, and some of the events from their annual meeting were captured in photographs between 1954 and 1956. The S.C.C.T.S.D. published three to four bulletins per year for members, starting in 1957.

The annual meetings became Annual Conventions in 1961. Other changes occurred for S.C.C.T.S.D. in the 1960s as well. The bulletins, for instance, were given the title, Sacred Doctrine Notes as of January 1966. Then, S.C.C.T.S.D. changed its name to the College Theology Society in 1968 so as to encourage an ecumenical dialogue with teachers in religious studies and theology. To this end, CTS renamed the bulletin in 1968 to College Theology Notes, only to be replaced one year later as the CTS Presidential Newsletter. Meanwhile, the Second Vatican Council held the concerns of CTS members on issues relating to academic freedom. CTS responded to issues on academic freedom with documents such as the Land O'Lakes statements. Other noteworthy events that occurred at this time for CTS include the Charles Curran case at the Catholic University of America and new theological movements such as liberation theology.

CTS began to foster relationships with other theological societies in the 1970s, including the Joint Committee of Catholic Learned Societies and Scholars, the International Federation of Catholic Universities, and the Council on the Study of Religion. CTS presidents made a special effort to build relationships with bishops so as to strengthen the tie between clerics and teachers at Catholic colleges and universities. Publishing was increasing important to the Society, which incited CTS in 1974 to create the journal, Horizons.

New committees formed within CTS in the 1980s. The Women's Caucus, for example, tackled controversial issues that involved the degree of women's participation in the ministry and life of the Catholic Church. Old controversies arose for subjects that CTS considered at this time as well. The Charles Curran case resurged, and Cardinal Ratzinger criticized liberation theology as problematic to the Church. Meanwhile, the CTS Publication Series began in the 1980s, which analyzed topics such as abortion so as to determine a way that teachers could approach current trends in the classroom. CTS offered new publishing opportunities to combine the study of religion with other academic disciplines such as anthropology to meet the needs of students.

In the 1990s, CTS continued to discuss issues that were related to theologians' formal relationship with the Roman Catholic Church. The principal topic of debate was on John Paul's Ex corde Ecclesiae, particularly canon 812 which required theologians to receive mandatum from his or her local bishop. The mandatum debate was fully engaged at the 2000 CTS Annual Convention. As an active organization, CTS continues to tackle controversial issues that are relevant to theologians at colleges and universities today.

Scope and Contents

The records of the College Theology Society are divided into seven series. The first series in box 1 consists of the constitution and by-laws of CTS from 1954 to 2015. The second series in boxes 1-2 contains agendas and minutes from the Board of Directors meetings with the files organized in chronological order. Related certificates and notes taken by the board members are included.

The third series in boxes 2-10 consists of general administration files that is divided into ten sub-series. The first sub-series has material from the annual business and regional meetings between 1960 and 2013, which includes some of the agendas and procedures from both meetings. The second sub-series consists of reports from a variety of areas such as publications, geographical regions, officers, and Annual Conventions. The third sub-series has records from the committees of CTS between 1957 and 2011. The fourth sub-series consists of records on the theological societies that interacted with CTS between 1953 and 2007 such as the Council on the Study of Religion, the International Federation of Catholic Universities, and Joint Committee of Catholic Learned Societies and Scholars. The fifth sub-series covers the activity from members within each of the geographical regions of CTS from 1954-1996. There are extensive records from eastern Pennsylvania, Louisville, and Washington-Maryland regions. The sixth sub-series has records on CTS members and issues relating to membership between 1954-2002. There are lists of members from 1955-2002 in this sub-subseries as well. Records include address books, regional mailing lists, member directories, surveys for a new directory, and non-member colleges. The seventh sub-series contains financial records from 1959-2010. It has an account book, as well as transactions from the CTS bank account, annual meetings and conventions, theological societies, regional expenses, and Internal Revenue Service. The eighth sub-series contains some of the awards and certificates from 1972-2005 that were given for academic papers which were published in the CTS Annual Volume and presented at an Annual Convention. This sub-series also includes handwritten notes and graduate student essay flyers. The ninth sub-series contains records on the elections and nominations of CTS offices such as the president, vice president, secretary, and treasurer. The tenth sub-series consists of the secretary's data between 1953 and 1997, which has assorted documents from binders that the secretary assembled.

The fourth series consists of correspondence from 1953-2012 in boxes 10-15. It is arranged chronologically and then alphabetically. This series includes correspondence on the Annual Conventions, committees, finances, membership, publications, and other theological societies. There is regional correspondence with extensive records from the Chicago, eastern Pennsylvania, and Washington-Maryland regions. The forth series also has correspondence from officers such as the CTS president, vice president, and secretary.

The fifth series in boxes 15-16 contains programs, brochures, booklets, and general information on CTS Annual Conventions from 1955 to 2013.

The sixth series has records on publications in boxes 16-23. It include materials on topics such as academic freedom and copyright, as well as editorial resources from publishing houses such as the University Press of America, Orbis Publishing Company, and Scholars Press. Series six also contains the CTS bulletins from 1957 until the bulletin was renamed to Sacred Doctrine Notes and eventually became the presidential newsletter. There are also newsletters from the eastern Pennsylvania region, the CTS Notes which was distributed in the 1970s, and bulletins from theological societies that interacted with CTS. Other publications include the CTS journal Horizons and the CTS Publication Series that features writers such as Joseph A. Bracken during the 1980s. There are a number of published articles and newspaper clippings in this series as well, that document controversial issues such as the Charles Curran case. Finally, series six contains a number of books by CTS members, in addition to the proceedings from the First Annual Convention of the Society of the Catholic College Teachers of Sacred Doctrine (1955-1965) and most of the Annual Volumes until 2012.

The seventh and final series, Audio-Visual, in box 23 has disks, diskettes, and photographs. The disks and diskettes have material such as academic papers, CTS logos, and presentations given at the CTS Annual Convention in 2002. The photographs are mostly from 1954-1956.

Finally, there is a small addendum of recently arrived materials, ca. 1991-2015, to be interfiled with the collection at a later date.
