Descriptive Summary

Creator: The Catholic Theological Society of America
Title: The Catholic Theological Society of America Records
Dates: 1946-2016
Abstract: The Catholic Theological Society of America (CTSA) was formed in New York City in 1946. The CTSA is a professional organization that works to promote studies and research in theology, to relate theological reflection to current problems, and to raise the professional standards of seminary teaching. The records provide necessary background material from the Society's activities, showing the its evolution from being primarily an association of seminary professors to being a society open to religious, laity, and clerics without distinction. They include correspondence, minutes, reports, publications, and financial records.
Extent: 37.5 linear feet; 30 boxes
Language: English
Identification: 88

No documents of living persons from the previous twenty-five (25) years may be quoted without the permission of the person or persons concerned. Historical Note

A group of Catholic University of America (CUA) theological faculty members saw the need in the fall of 1945 for a professional association of Catholic theologians similar to the existing associations of philosophers, historians, sociologists, and biblical scholars. The group obtained the episcopal approval and patronage of the Archbishop of New York. It is legally incorporated in the State of New York as a non-profit corporation. With the appointment of temporary officers, the Society convened its first annual meeting in June 1946 in New York City.

The Board of Directors oversees the Society and consists of ten members who serve two year terms. These are the current president, the president-elect, the vice president, the secretary, the treasurer, the immediate past president, and four others. At each annual convention a new vice-president is elected who then moves on to become president-elect and president. Among the notable presidents have been Francis J. Connell in 1946-1947, Charles Curran in 1969-1970, Carl J. Peter in 1971-1972, Avery Dulles in 1975-1976, and Monika K. Hellwig in 1986-1987. There are standing committees for nomination, admissions, and resolutions. In addition, there are ad hoc committees on special research projects and cooperation with the hierarchy or other professional associations.

After Vatican Council II the Society changed the academic requirements for membership. This change allowed more religious and laity to apply for membership. The subjects treated at the annual meetings broadened from those related to a seminary curriculum to all phases of life. With a decline in the study of Latin has come the concern that many theologians no longer have the tools of access to their Catholic theological traditions. The Society has striven for greater cooperation with the National Conference of Catholic Bishops and in particular with its Committee on Doctrine.

The society publishes its annual proceedings as well as special study projects such as Women in Church and Society in 1978 and the Report on the Profession of Faith and Oath of Fidelity in 1990.

Scope and Contents

This is a finding aid following the minimal processing standards of More Product Less Process (MPLP) used in order to promote greater access to this valuable collection.

The first series contains Board of Directors Records 1951(1958-1992)1992 in boxes 1-3. The records have been divided into four subseries. The first subseries is the CTSA Constitution and it Bylaws (1953-1982). The second subseries has correspondence, 1951-1992, and some news clippings. The third subseries contains the Board's meeting minutes and exhibits, 1960-1992. The files include one bound ledger with handwritten minutes. Topics of the meetings of the Committee on Current Problems are Morality of Gambling, Laws of Fast and Abstinence, The Morality of Pre-Frontal Lobotomy, The Moral Problems of Biological Warfare, Governmental Suppression of Subversive Organizations, Morality of the Truth Serum in Criminal Investigations, Relationships with Non-Catholics in the Light of Recent Papal Directives. The fourth subseries consists of committee files, 1946-1992, that are sorted by committee name and date.

The second series contains the executive secretary correspondence of Luke C. Salm, 1976-1985. Other secretary correspondence is filed in the third series, Elected CTSA Officers' Records.

The third series includes Elected CTSA Officers' Records, 1945-1992 and is divided into four subseries. The first subseries has Mixed Officers' Correspondence, 1945-1964. The second subseries contains Individual Officers' Records, including Presidents' Correspondence, sorted chronologically by name of President, and file boxes of membership cards. The third subseries contains Secretaries' Records, including Correspondence, Membership Records, and Directories. The fourth subseries is the Treasurers' Records and is sorted chronologically by Treasurers' name. These records include correspondence, ledgers, bank stubs, and financial reports.

The fourth series contains Convention Records, 1946-1992, and is divided into two subseries. The first subseries is Convention Correspondence, 1945-1992, including documents from the International Congress of Learned Societies in the Field of Religion. These files also contain three copies of a convention program: Religion and the Humanizing of Man (Los Angeles, 1972). The second subseries contains the records for the periodical "Proceedings" (1949-1991). These records include Editorial Correspondence, 1949-1991; Copyright Correspondence, 1952-1977; Book Reviews, 1967-1984; and copies of "Proceedings," volumes 1-46, (see addendum). The annual convention progressed from a few major papers to an elaborate program of plenary addresses, seminars, workshops, and special interest functions. It became the vice-president's responsibility to plan the annual convention. These records will be found in the third series, Elected Officers' Records, under the individual's name.

The fifth series is Regional Meeting Records, 1955-1985, including Annual Reports and Correspondence. These records are arranged alphabetically by place.

The sixth series is the Addendum of miscellaneous and unprocessed records, 1950-2015, of the multiple accessions received since 1992 from various officers and stored in boxes 11-30. Additional record deposits are expected and the material in this series will hopefully be processed in greater detail at a future date. The aforementioned convention proceedings are herein as well.

The seventh series is Electronic Records, 1946(2010-2016)2016. These include primarily Word documents and PDFs, but also some Excel sheets and JPEGs. These include PDFs of the Convention Proceedings, 1946-2011, and administrative materials such as correspondence, applications, minutes, programs, reports, budgets, for 2010-2016. Access questions can be directed to archives@mail.lib.cua.edu.
