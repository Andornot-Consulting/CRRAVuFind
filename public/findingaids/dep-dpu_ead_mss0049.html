Biographical Note:

Anne M. Burke, Justice, Illinois Appellate Court, First District, is an alumna of DePaul and has served on DePaul University's Board of Trustees since 1999. Her interest in and concern for the rights of children in society began in 1965, when she was one of the first to teach physical education for mentally challenged children through the Chicago Park District. She began her judicial career in 1987 on the Illinois Court of Claims. She was appointed Special Counsel for Child Welfare Services in 1994. She continued her advocacy for children's issues in her service on the National Review Board for the Protection of Children and Young People (NRB), appointed by the United States Conference of Catholic Bishops (USCCB) of the U.S. Roman Catholic Church to study issues raised in the clergy sex abuse scandal. She served as interim chair of the NRB from 2002 to 2004.

Also included are files of Justice Burke’s speeches she gave about her role as Interim Chair and publications she collected about the clergy sex abuse scandal.

Series 001: John Jay Study 2003-2004

This series contains files on and related to a study that was both issued and sponsored by the National Review Board (NRB) of the United States Conference of Catholic Bishops (USSCB) and conducted by the John Jay College of Criminal Justice: The Nature and Scope of Sexual Abuse of Minors by Catholic Priests and Deacons in the United States 1950-2002. Completed in February 2004, this study, mandated from the USCCB, called for a comprehensive investigation of the prevalence and incidence of sexual abuse of minors under the age of 18 by Catholic priests and deacons between 1950 and 2002, and of its costs as measured by financial settlements and legal actions. The files in this series primarily consist of a study proposal, progress reports, general correspondence, and various media publications.

Series 002: National Review Board 2002-2006

The National Review Board (NRB) Files series comprises the largest portion of the Anne Burke Papers; moreover, a majority of these files are in the Subject Files subseries. The NRB was established by the United States Conference of Catholic Bishops (USCCB) during their meeting in June 2002 and its purpose is to collaborate with the USCCB in preventing the sexual abuse of minors in the United States by persons in the service of the Catholic Church. The files in this series consist of meeting minutes, subcommittee meeting minutes, administrative and biographical files, as well as subject files comprised of various publications. The files in the Membership subseries are divided into two headings: Bio Files and New Members. The files in the first heading are arranged alphabetically and the latter is arranged in chronological order

This series is subdivided into six subseries: Administrative Files, Membership, Meetings, Rome Trip, Subcommittees, Subject Files. The subseries are arranged chronologically or alphabetically.

Series 004: Office of Children and Youth Protection Files 2002-2005

This series contains files pertaining to the Office of Children and Youth Protection (OCYP). The OCYP was established by the United States Conference of Catholic Bishops (USCCB) at their meeting in Dallas, Texas in June 2002 and is responsible for assisting dioceses and eparchies in the United States in the implementation of safe environment programs to protect children. The OCYP also oversees an audit process which measures the progress of all dioceses and eparchies in complying with the Charter for the Protection of Children and Young People in addition to assisting the NRB in the conduct of their duties. The files in this series mainly consist of administrative files, reports, correspondence, and audit files.

This series is arranged chronologically with the exception of files about the OCYP's establishment at the beginning of the series.
