Biographical Note

Since 1972, St. Vincent DePaul Center has offered services such as childcare, senior services, homeless services, food distribution, social assistance, and therapy. The Center represents a merger between St. Vincent Infant Asylum, a more than 100-year-old residential facility for orphans, and DePaul Settlement and Day Nursery. The DePaul Settlement and Day Nursery was founded in 1915 by the Daughters of Charity of St. Vincent DePaul.

Series 001: DePaul Settlement House and Day Nursery, St. Vincent's Infant Asylum/St. Vincent's Orphanage, and St. Vincent de Paul Center 1897-2007

The files in Series 1 include documents collected, created, or maintained in the DePaul University Archives and Lincoln Park neighborhood collections, or transferred by individuals directly associated with the St. Vincent de Paul Center or its predecessor organizations. The materials are limited in scope. There are materials that document the origins of the DePaul Settlement House and Day Nursery, correspondence with neighborhood organizations requesting financial support, annual reports, brochures, flyers, newsletters, programs, and photographs.

Series 002: Photographs 1909-1990s

The photographs in Series 2 were acquired by Rev. Edward Udovic, C.M., University Mission and Values. Fr. Udovic began collecting and purchasing Chicago and Lincoln Park ephemera, material culture, and photographs for DePaul University beginning in 2002, relying mostly on online auction sites such as ebay.com. To aid researchers, the photographs and negatives Fr. Udovic purchased that document the activities of the DePaul Settlement House and Day Nursery, St. Vincent's Infant Asylum/St. Vincent's Orphanage, and St. Vincent de Paul Center were added to this collection.
