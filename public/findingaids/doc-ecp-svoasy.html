Background

St. Vincent's Orphan Asylum at Randolph Street was founded by the Daughters of Charity on June 5, 1851. In 1867, the boys were transferred to St. Anthony's Asylum. The Asylum moved to McDougall Avenue eight years later. By 1943, all children except those in first and second grade attended outside schools. By 1948 the neighborhood had declined and the building had been condemned. In July of that year, the Asylum merged with Sarah Fisher Home in Farmington Hills.
