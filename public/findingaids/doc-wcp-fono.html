Background

The Daughters of Charity first arrived in New Orleans in 1830 to work at the Poydras Asylum, an orphanage run by a combination of Catholic and Protestant laywomen. In 1836, the Daughters withdrew from Poydras and started their own orphanage. It was originally known as St. Patrick's before being incorporated as the New Orleans Female Orphan Asylum. Girls older than 14 were separated in 1855 and sent to the newly established St. Elizabeth's House of Industry for vocational training. The Female Orphan Asylum merged with St. Elizabeth's in 1921, and the reunited institution officially became incorporated as St. EliZabeth's in 1955.
