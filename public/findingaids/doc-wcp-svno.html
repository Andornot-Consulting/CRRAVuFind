Background

St. Vincent's Infant Home was started in 1858 to care for an increased number of orphans following yellow fever and cholera epidemics. It originally served only babies and toddlers, with older children transferring to other institutions. In 1869, a maternity program was added. Other services were added later, including residential and outpatient care for unwed mothers. St. Vincent's closed in 1987.
