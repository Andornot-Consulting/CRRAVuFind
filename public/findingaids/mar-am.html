Biography of Anna McGarry

Anna McGarry was born in Philadelphia on March 17, 1894, one of eight children in an Irish-Catholic family. She married Frank McGarry in 1917, but his death four years later left her widowed with a young daughter. During the 1920s McGarry became increasingly aware of racial injustice in Philadelphia, and she devoted the rest of her life to improving interracial relations.

In 1934 McGarry helped to found the West Philadelphia Interracial Forum. In 1937 she helped organize the Catholic Intercollegiate Interracial Council, which later became the Catholic Interracial Council of Philadelphia. In 1945 McGarry retired from her secretarial job to become the night house mother at a school of nursing, a move which allowed her to devote her days to lobbying the city for policies that promoted racial justice. In 1946 McGarry became a part-time field representative of Philadelphia's new Fair Employment Practices commission. The agency re-formed in 1952 to become the Commission on Human Relations, and McGarry served as a full-time staff member.

McGarry retired from public service in 1959, but she remained active in the National Catholic Social Action Conference and the National Catholic Council for Interracial Justice. She spent her final years living with her daughter in Loveland, Ohio and died there on January 3, 1978.
