Biography of Rev. George H. Tavard, A.A.

George Henry Tavard was born in Nancy, France in 1922. He was ordained into the Augustinians of the Assumption at Metz in 1947. In 1949 Tavard received a Doctorate of Sacred Theology from Facults Catholiques de Lyon. He moved to the United States in the 1950s where he enjoyed a long and distinguished teaching career, becoming a leading scholar of ecumenism. Tavard taught theology at a variety of institutions in the U.S., including (but not limited to) Assumption College in Worcester MA (1958-1959), Mount Mercy College in Pittsburgh (1960-1966), and Pennsylvania State University (1967-1969). He went on to teach at the Methodist Theological School of Deleware for 19 years, retiring in 1990. Tavard came out of retirement in 1992 to hold the Presidential Chair in Theology at Marquette University, a position he held until 1995.

From 1960-1970, Tavard served on the Pontifical Secretariat for Christian Unity. In 1962 Pope John XXIII appointed him a theological "peritus" (expert advisor to the bishops) at the Second Vatican Council. Tavard's reputation as an ecumenist led to later involvement in bilateral dialogue groups, such as the Anglican-Roman Catholic International Conversation and the Lutheran Catholic Dialogue in the United States.

Tavard published nearly 50 books and 250 articles during his career. In addition to being regarded as a foremost ecumenist, Tavard was an expert on Bonaventure, and he was one of the first Catholic theologians to raise the issue of women's roles in Christian tradition.

Tavard died in Paris in August 2007.

Scope and Contents of the Collection

Includes manuscripts for published and unpublished works; professional and personal correspondence; printed and audio recordings of lectures and speeches; published articles and book reviews. Also includes records from his assignment as a "peritus" during the Second Vatican Council, including his involvement in the Pontifical Secretariat for Promoting Christian Unity and subsequent bilateral dialogues.
