Administrative History

In 1898 Mr. and Mrs. William Amberg established a Catholic mission called Guardian Angel Center in an Italian immigtant neighborhood on the near southwest side of Chicago. Their daughter, Mary Agnes Amberg (1874-1962), took over operation of the center in 1912 and transformed it into a settlement house, renaming it the Madonna Center in 1922. The Madonna Center would serve the local community until its closing in 1966.

The Madonna Center worked closely with another organization called the Christ Child Society of Chicago. Its mission was to serve poor and destitute children, particularly by providing articles of clothing and gifts at Christmastime. The Christ Child Society had been founded in 1907, and it would outlast the Madonna Center by little over a decade, disbanding in 1978.

Scope and Contents of the Collection

Included in this collection are correspondence, reports, case files, and related records. Also included are personal papers of Mary Agnes Amberg, the head resident, and records of the Christ Child Society of Chicago, with which Madonna Center was closely associated. Of note is the correspondence between Amberg and her long-time associate, Marie Plamendon, which reveals their close relationship.
