Administrative History

The Seminarians' Catholic Action movement was inspired by the Jocist (a.k.a. Young Christian Workers) movement founded by Rev. (later Cardinal ) Joseph Cardijn prior to the First World War. Rev. Thomas Reese helped to found the Seminarians' movement in the 1940s while a student at Catholic University's Theological College. The goal of the movement was to promote the theology of Catholic Action among seminary students in the hope they would become Catholic Action chaplains in their priesthood.

Scope and Contents of the Collection

Included in this collection are correspondence, manuscripts, publications, and reports that document the activities of study groups at the Theological College and other seminaries, and the gatherings held at the University of Notre Dame in 1946 and in Montreal in 1947. Also included are minutes of meetings of a Young Christian Workers "cell" in Wilmington, Delaware, 1946-1948.
