Background

The Diocese of Dubuque was erected in 1837 with Jean Mathias Peter Loras as first bishop, succeeded by T. Clement Smyth in 1857 and John Hennessy in 1866.

Return to the Table of Contents

Scope and Content

Letters sent by Bishops Loras and Hennessy; manuscripts of lectures by Bishop Loras; drawings of saints for the Dubuque Cathedral; and a Denkschrift of Adam Handl, Richmond (Washington County), Iowa, 1861.

CADU /5 Luigi Gregori drawings for Saint Raphael Cathedral in Dubuque c1886

12 drawings on 11 sheets: Saints Paul?, Dominic, Leo (Pope Leo I), Vincent de Paul, Jerome, Ambrose, Patrick, Francis of Assisi, Bernard (2), Thomas Aquinas, and John Chrysostom. Identifications are in Italian; what we think is St. Paul is not identified and has a bit different look to it than the others.

In four issues of Volume 20, the Notre Dame Scholastic notes that Gregori was working on the Cathedral in 1886: Issue 4, Issue 8, Issue 11, and Issue 13.

Issue 16 mentions Gregori's donation of 21 sketches to the Bishops Memorial Hall at Notre Dame in December 1886. A dozen of these sketches came to the archives. Notre Dame's Snite Museum of Art holds other sketches by Gregori for the cathedral in Dubuque.
