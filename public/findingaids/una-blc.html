Background

Professor of Religious Studies at Ursuline College in Pepper Pike near Cleveland, Ohio; member of the Cleveland Diocesan Pastoral Council for 2009-2011.

Return to the Table of Contents

Scope and Content

Research material gathered by Sister Dorothy Ann Blatnica for her doctoral dissertation, In Those Days: African American Catholics in Cleveland, 1922-1961 (Case Western Reserve University, 1992), published as At the altar of their God: African American Catholics in Cleveland, 1922-1961 (New York: Garland, 1995); consisting of transcriptions of interviews; photocopies of historical documents including correspondence, reports, and newspaper clippings; releases signed by subjects of interviews; the prospectus for her dissertation, with critiques; and a copy of the dissertation itself. Also audio recordings (micro cassettes) of interviews and photographic prints of historical and contemporary images of black Catholic parishes and parishioners in Cleveland, Ohio.
