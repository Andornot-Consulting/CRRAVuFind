Background

Msgr. Raymond T. Bosler served as pastor of St. John's Church in Indianapolis, Indiana, wrote The Question Box for Universal Press Syndicate, and served as a peritus during the Second Vatican Council.

Return to the Table of Contents

Scope and Content

Sermons, talks, lectures, days of renewal, newspaper columns (The Question Box) 1967-1977, STD dissertation St Thomas and the Secular Society (1947), manuscript of book New Wine Bursting Old Skins: Memories of an Old Priest Longing for a New Church, lithograph, and certificates; photographs including many from his 50th anniversary as a priest, some showing his classmates, and some from 1930s Europe; mortarboard, biretta, personal seal, award, relics with certificates, medallion of Indiana Academy membership; Latin books: Imitation of Christ, Denzinger's Enchiridion Symbolorum, Conciliorum Oecumenieorum Decreta, and The Story of Milhousen, Indiana, where Bosler served.
