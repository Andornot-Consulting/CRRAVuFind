Background

The Notre Dame Center for Ethics and Culture is (to quote its mission statement) a "center for scholarly reflection within the Catholic intellectual and moral tradition" which supports "appropriately focused scholarly research in ethics and its dissemination in the classroom and the broader culture."

Return to the Table of Contents

Scope and Content

Records of the Notre Dame Center for Ethics and Culture, including 94 VHS video tapes of conferences: "Culture of Death" (2000), "Culture of Life" (2001), "From Death to Life" (2002), "Formation and Renewal" (2003), "Epiphanies of Beauty" (2004), "J. Philip Clarke Medical Ethics Conferences" (1988, 1990, 1991, 1993-1997), "Catholic Culture Lecture Series" (2004), "Looking Back, Looking Forward: Ethical Reflections in a Changing Cultural Landscape" (2004), and "Arthur J. Schmitt Lecture Series" (2004); also photographs and digital data.
