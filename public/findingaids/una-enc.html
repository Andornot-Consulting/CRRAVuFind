Scope and Content

Letters from members of the American Catholic hierarchy to the Rectors of the English College in Rome, who acted as their agents; letters from bishops Henry Conwell, Ambrose Marechal, James Whitfield, Samuel Eccleston, and Edward Fenwick, and from Father Gabriel Richard; letters to rectors Robert Gradwell and Nicholas Patrick Wiseman.

In English, Latin, French, and Italian.

MENC BAL 100 Notes on the Table of Brics. (No. 99) written by Marechal. Dubourg's diocese of St. Louis would stretch to the Pacific and Russian America. Limits of Rosati's diocese of New Orleans. Make him temporary administrator of Alabama and Florida. Dubourg's influence on Flaget. His schemes for an Archbpric. Neale opposed him, but Litts supported him. Grassi proposed for Detroit, but needed in Rome. Ben Fenwick instead. However Detroit not needed yet and Fenwick not suitable as it is a French speaking area. Hopes of converting the savages largely dreams. Difficulties arise through contact with Europeans, the sale of strong drink; Dubourg, Rosati, Flaget, L. Neale, Litta, Grassi, Ben Fenwick
