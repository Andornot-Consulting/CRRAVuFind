Background

Archbishop of San Antonio, 1941-1969; implemented papal directives on social justice, Catholic Action and liturgical renewal. In 1945 the U.S. bishops established a Committee for the Spanish-Speaking, with its permanent headquarters in San Antonio, designed to provide for the religious, social, economic, educational and cultural advancement of Mexican-Americans. In 1964 it merged with the Bishops' Committee for Migrant Workers.

Return to the Table of Contents

Scope and Content

Correspondence concerning the Vietnam War, Lucey's trip to South Vietnam as an observer of the 1967 election, his friendship with Lyndon Johnson, his participation in the Second Vatican Council, the papal encyclical Humanae Vitae, civil rights, Latin America, and the plight of the migrant farm worker; records relating to the archbishop's residence in San Antonio; sermons, invitations, programs, clippings and memorabilia from anniversary celebrations; and records of the Bishops' Committee for the Spanish Speaking (1944-1970), the Catholic Council for the Spanish Speaking (1946-1962), the Bishops' Committee for Catholic Migrant Labor (1952-1953), and the Confraternity of Christian Doctrine (1941-1970).

Available in microfilm (22 reels)

MLUC Robert Emmet Lucey: Microfilm

MLUC 1 Reel One

Photographs, correspondence, sermons, programs, expenses, clippings re 40th anniversary of his priesthood, 1956; his 25th silver episcopal jubilee, 1959; triple jubilee banquet, 1966, program included Cardinals Spellman and Sheehan; biographical sketch. (1966) at 091. (CLUC 1/01 to 1/07)

MLUC 2 Reel Two

Continued from reel 1. Photograph with President Johnson at 111, birthday greetings, Lucey coat of arms, family history correspondence, dismissorial letters. (CLUC 1/08 to 1/11)

MLUC 3 Reel Three

Continued from reel 2 without indexing. Entertainment details, guest lists, testimonial dinner program with highlights of Lucey's life, including the delivery of the invocation at the inauguration of President Johnson, Christmas cards, gifts. (CLUC 2/01 to 4/01)

MLUC 4 Reel Four

Continued from reel 3, same. Easter cards and gifts. (CLUC 4/02 to 5/02)

MLUC 5 Reel Five

Personal correspondence, all subjects, including Vietnam war, U.S.S. Pueblo, United Farm workers, radicals, personal appeals. (CLUC 5/03 to 6/10)

MLUC 6 Reel Six

White House dinner details, other invitations, funeral and investiture notices, schedules and itineraries, correspondence re gifts and invitations. (CLUC 7/01 to 7/06)

MLUC 7 Reel Seven

Miscellaneous correspondence including requests for aid, Bishop Sheen, Vatican II reforms, James A. Farley, criticism of the Vietnam War, Lucey's trip to Vietnam, Henry B. Gonzales MC, farm workers. (CLUC 8/01 to 9/05)

MLUC 8 Reel Eight

Personal correspondence, continued, including Humanae Vitae controversy, Fr. James S. Brandes. Many photos after 176. File of correspondence with President Johnson 1948-1969, Bess Truman letter 1948, press releases, business matters re church furnishings. (CLUC 9/06 to 10/07)

MLUC 9 Reel Nine

Business correspondence re the Archbishop's residence, entertaining, retirement (1969). (CLUC 10/08 to 11/09)

MLUC 10 Reel Ten

Continued from reel 9. Retirement, correspondence re Lucey's attendance at Vatican II council in Rome and other travels, as observer of 1967 elections in Vietnam, criticism of the war, clippings, note from President Johnson, Lucey's support of the war, analysis of 1967 RVN elections at 151, notes from 9-8-67 news conference, photos from Vietnam trip. (CLUC 11/09 to 13/01)

MLUC 11 Reel Eleven

Personal correspondence 1954-57, vestment catalogues and orders, automobiles, charity fund, tax matters, apparel purchases. (CLUC 13/01 to 13/07)

MLUC 12 Reel Twelve

Bishops' Committee for the Spanish Speaking (BCSS) annual reports and correspondence 1944-1969. (CLUC 13/08 to 14/07)

MLUC 13 Reel Thirteen

Continued from reel 12. BCSS correspondence. (CLUC 14/07 to 16/03)

MLUC 14 Reel Fourteen

Continued from reel 13. Includes correspondence with the Apostolic Delegation to the U.S. re BCSS. (CLUC 16/03 to 17/02)

MLUC 15 Reel Fifteen

Continued from reel 14. BCSS correspondence, including legal agreements, donations, budgets, monthly reports of U.S. Catholic Conference re BCSS, activities on behalf of farm worker organization in the southwest. (CLUC 17/03 to 18/04)

MLUC 16 Reel Sixteen

Continued from reel 15. General correspondence 1946-56, Bishops' Committee for Catholic Migrant labor, Confraternity of Christian Doctrine correspondence to and from Archbishop Lucey. (CLUC 18/05 to 19/06)

MLUC 17 Reel Seventeen

Continued from reel 16. CCD meeting minutes, correspondence, Baltimore catechism revision, correspondence of various CCD committees. (CLUC 19/07 to 20/10)

MLUC 18 Reel Eighteen

Continued from reel 17. CCD correspondence re Italy and Latin America, Lucey's 1968 trip to Colombia. (CLUC 21/01 to 21/12)

MLUC 19 Reel Nineteen

Continued from reel 18. CCD correspondence with Rev Ramon Garcia from many Latin American countries re work of CCD. (CLUC 22/01 to 22/04)

MLUC 20 Reel Twenty

Continued from reel 19. CCD correspondence re Latin America with Rev. John H. Gorham and Rev. James F. McNiff. (CLUC 23/01 to 24/02)

MLUC 21 Reel Twenty-one

Continued from reel 20. CCD correspondence with Latin American representatives, many in spanish; chronology of CCD activities in Latin America 1941-1959 at 078. (CLUC 24/03 to 25/06)

MLUC 22 Reel Twenty-two

CCD correspondence with Latin America, financial reports. (CLUC 25/07 to 25/15)
