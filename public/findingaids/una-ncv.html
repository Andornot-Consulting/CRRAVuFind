Background

Vicars for Religious are diocesan officers in charge of relations between the diocese and the congregations of religious within it (religious sisters, brothers, and priests).

Return to the Table of Contents

Scope and Content

Correspondence, minutes, newsletters, printed circulars, programs, papers, reports, guidelines, resource directories, membership lists, mailings, and mission statements: including documents (1967-1968) from the early days of the National Conference of Vicars for Religious: Records and notes from NCVR Annual Assemblies (1970- ), Executive Board minutes; NCVR secretary's files (1970- ), including correspondence of secretary with treasurer; reading files (Prior to 1986, 1986-1989); financial files (1986-1989); billings (1994-1998); midwest regional minutes; printed material on NCVR and other printed material.

Also correspondence files of Rev. Robert Nash with organizations and individuals including the Leadership Conference of Women Religious, the Conference of Major Superiors of Men, the Vatican Congregation for Religious, the Association of Contemplative Sisters, bishops, the National Assembly of Religious Brothers, the Apostolic Pro-Nuncio, and other vicars. Nash served as President of NCVR (1986-1990).
