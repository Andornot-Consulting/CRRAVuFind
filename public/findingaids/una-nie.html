Background

Professor of botany and organic chemistry, University of Notre Dame, 1904-1936; inventor of neoprene (synthetic rubber).

Return to the Table of Contents

Scope and Content

Correspondence, 1895-1936, concerning his botanical studies, including his work on the American Midland Naturalist, and his chemical studies, including correspondence with researchers at Du Pont, such as W.H. Carothers and W.S. Calcott, documenting his progress in perfecting the formula for neoprene; botany lecture notes, notes taken by Nieuwland as a student, manuscripts of works by Nieuwland, pamphlets, patents by Nieuwland, receipts, clippings, a journal; manuscripts of works about Nieuwland such as "Julius A. Nieuwland, C.S.C.: The Man, the Priest, the Scientist" by Richard Rutherford and "Father Nieuwland and Neoprene: A Retrospective Reassessment" by Herman Schroeder; correspondence and material concerning the J.A. Nieuwland Memorial Exercises, 1937; and photographs.

PNIE Julius A. Nieuwland: Printed Material

Botany tracts (1830s-1900s) collected and bound by Fr Nieuwland in 97 numbered volumes some of which are missing. The individual pamphlets, periodicals, articles, reports, and papers in these have been cataloged and can be found through Notre Dame's library catalog. Chiefly English, many German and French, some other languages. Topics extend to anything of interest to a turn-of-the-century naturalist (e.g., birds, geology, agriculture).
