Background

Starting as an instructor in economic history in 1931, and continuing until 1973, Wesley C. Bender served as a teacher of marketing and business administration at the University of Notre Dame who in 1943 became a full professor and head of the department. After 1947 he also served as a member of the Academic Council of the University.

Return to the Table of Contents

Scope and Content

Documentation of his career at Notre Dame, including coursework and administrative files; of his professional life, including academic and research material and files representing his membership in organizations; personal papers including correspondence, family papers, papers from his education in high school, at Cornell University, and at Northwestern University, certificates, and diplomas; a scrapbook containing clippings about him or his family and some correspondence; medical and financial records of Dr. Walter H. Baker; and closed files of student papers, grade records, and letters of recommendation.
